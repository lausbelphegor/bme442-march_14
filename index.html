Quantitative Analysis of Prion Disease Using an AI-Powered Digital Pathology Framework

Welcome! Today, I will guide you through how this study leveraged both machine learning and deep learning techniques and enhanced the accuracy and efficiency of diagnosing prion diseases.

Study Aim

To develop an AI-based framework for the accurate classification of Whole Slide Images (WSIs) as positive or negative for prion disease and quantify prion proteins' distribution across tissue sections.

The primary goal of the study is to leverage AI to improve the diagnostic process for prion diseases, potentially extending this methodology to other neurodegenerative disorders.

Dataset Overview

Source: Piedmont Reference Center for Diagnosis and Monitoring of Prion Diseases (DOMP)

  • 41 patients
  • WSIs from cerebellum and occipital lobe
  • Collected between 2002 and 2008
The dataset was obtained from the Piedmont Reference Center for Diagnosis and Monitoring of Prion Diseases (DOMP). It includes WSIs from the cerebellum and occipital lobe of 41 patients, collected between 2002 and 2008. The immunohistochemistry staining with the 12F10 antibody highlights protein aggregates in red.
