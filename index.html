Quantitative Analysis of Prion Disease Using an AI-Powered Digital Pathology Framework

Massimo Salvi, Filippo Molinari, Mario Ciccarelli, Roberto Testi, Stefano Taraglio, Daniele Imperiale

Politecnico di Torino, Turin, Italy

The study aimed to develop an automated pipeline for the identification of prion protein aggregates (PrPSc) in tissue samples from the cerebellar and occipital cortex using artificial intelligence (AI) powered by digital pathology. This framework intended to accurately classify whole slide images (WSIs) from patients definitively diagnosed with prion disease, quantify PrPSc distribution and localization throughout the brain, and potentially extend this method to evaluate protein expression in other neurodegenerative diseases like Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s.
